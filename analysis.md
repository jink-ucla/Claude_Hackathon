# Analysis — CD4⁺ T cell regulator landscape from genome-scale Perturb-seq

**A single integrated, honestly-scored view of which genes rewire the human CD4⁺ T cell
transcriptome when perturbed — stratified by stimulation state, and connected to helper-cell
polarization, aging, and autoimmune-disease genetics.**

Built entirely from the processed, publicly-shipped tables of the Marson/Pritchard 2025
genome-scale CRISPRi Perturb-seq screen in primary human CD4⁺ T cells (bioRxiv 2025,
[10.64898/2025.12.23.696273](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.64898/2025.12.23.696273v1);
data GitHub `emdann/GWT_perturbseq_analysis_2025`). Every number below is regenerated by
`scripts/build_dataset.py`; the biology is grounded in `docs/GROUNDING.md` and the companion
research dossier (`docs/CD4_perturbseq_research_dossier.md`).

---

## 1. What we integrated

The screen knocked down all expressed genes across ~22 million CD4⁺ T cells from 4 donors under
3 stimulation conditions, then measured the genome-wide transcriptional consequence of each
perturbation. That produces many *separate* derived tables — differential expression, guide
efficiency, reproducibility, gene-regulatory-network modules, disease enrichment — none of which,
alone, tells you which gene is a high-confidence, disease-relevant regulator. We joined **eight
evidence layers** into one master table of **33,983 perturbation × condition rows (11,526 unique
genes)**:

| Layer | Question it answers | Source table |
|---|---|---|
| **DE / trans-effect breadth** | How much does knocking it down reshape the transcriptome? | `DE_stats.suppl_table.csv` |
| **On-target validity** | Did CRISPRi actually knock the target down? | `DE_stats` + `guide_kd_efficiency` |
| **Cross-guide / cross-donor reproducibility** | Is the effect real, not a single-guide/donor artifact? | `DE_by_guide.*` / `DE_donor_robustness_*` |
| **GRN module membership** | Which regulatory program does it belong to? | `clustering_results_and_annotations.csv` |
| **Autoimmune-disease enrichment** | Is its downstream program enriched for disease genes? | `cluster_autoimmune_enrichment_results.*` |
| **Polarization / activation role** | Does it control Th differentiation / activation? | `polarization_prediction_..._coefficients.csv` |
| **Aging role** | Does it drive the CD4⁺ aging signature? | `aging_prediction_..._coefficients.csv` |
| **K562 cross-cell-type specificity** | Is the effect CD4-specific or shared with K562? | `K562_comparison.suppl_table.csv` |

The integration is the point: these layers are largely **complementary**. A regulator supported
by *several at once* — broad trans-effects **and** reproducible **and** a known polarization
regulator **and** sitting in an autoimmune-enriched module — is unusually interesting, and the
`regulator_score` is designed to surface exactly that convergence.

**Context-specificity is enforced by design.** The paper's central finding is that active
regulators change across stimulation states, so the master table is keyed by **gene × condition**
(Rest / Stim8hr / Stim48hr) and is *never* pooled.

## 2. The screen recovers canonical CD4⁺ biology (a built-in sanity benchmark)

Before trusting novel hits, the ranking should recover regulators we already know. It does:

- **The broadest trans-effect hubs at Stim8hr are the proximal TCR-signaling machinery** —
  **TADA2B** (SAGA), **CD3E (5,710 downstream genes), LAT (5,535), PLCG1 (5,032), ZAP70
  (5,021)** — exactly the genes whose loss should collapse the entire activation program.
- **The two perfect-scoring (9/9) regulators are BATF** (Th17-differentiation module) and
  **BAHD1** (MLL2/chromatin) — BATF is a textbook master TF of helper-cell cytokine programs.
- **Known regulators score high in order:** BATF 9; STAT3, STAT6, GATA3, RASA2 all 8; CBLB 7;
  TBX21 (T-bet), VAV1, MAP4K1 6. **GATA3 — the Th2 master TF — scores in the top tier (8) at both
  Rest and Stim48hr**, with its broadest trans-effects at Stim48hr (1,252 downstream genes).

**Honest counter-signal (a feature, not a bug):** the lineage-definers **FOXP3 (score 2) and
RORC (1)** rank *low*. Under these culture conditions the screen captures activation and
Th1/Th2/Th17-*cytokine* circuitry far better than the Treg/Th17 lineage-commitment programs,
which require specific polarizing cues not fully represented here. The score reflects *evidence
present in this dataset*, not ground-truth importance — the same honesty caveat that applies to
any convergent-evidence heuristic.

## 3. Prioritized CD4⁺ regulators (the headline output)

Sorting the master table by `regulator_score` (0–9; additive across the independent evidence
layers — see §6) surfaces a shortlist whose top of each condition is dominated by chromatin,
transcription-elongation, and TCR-signaling regulators:

| Condition | Top regulators (by score, then breadth) | Reads as |
|---|---|---|
| **Rest** | DHX36, DENR, STRAP, MAEA, CREBBP, MTA2, CHD4 | translation + chromatin remodelers (NuRD: MTA2/CHD4) |
| **Stim8hr** | **BATF**, BAHD1, EIF4G2, WAC, USP22, ATXN7L3, TRIP12 | Th17/cytokine TF + SAGA-DUB (USP22/ATXN7L3) |
| **Stim48hr** | BHLHE40, WAC, PDCL, **GATA3**, MTA2, PCGF1, RNF20 | late activation + Th2 master TF |

Across all conditions, **1,744 gene × condition rows score ≥ 6, 530 score ≥ 7**, and 2 reach the

(`figures/fig6_regulator_module_network.png` + `regulator_module_network.graphml` for
Cytoscape/Gephi) maps the elite (score ≥ 8) regulators onto the transcriptional complexes they
belong to — SAGA, Integrator, INO80, BAF, NCOR/HDAC, MLL2, PAF1, CHD4/MTA2.

## 4. Perturbation effects are strongly context-specific

The transcriptional consequence of the *same* perturbation differs across stimulation states —
the paper's core thesis, visible directly in the integrated table:

- On-target-significant perturbations rise from **6,869 (Rest) → 7,154 (Stim8hr) → 7,193
  (Stim48hr)**, and high-confidence regulators (score ≥ 6) peak at Stim8hr (660 vs 507 / 577).
- **Autoimmune-disease-enriched GRN modules (OR>1, FDR<0.1) are most numerous at Stim8hr
  (13 modules), closely followed by Stim48hr (9), with Rest near-zero (1)** — a real but modest
  context signal peaking in the activation window. (At the membership-weighted gene × condition
  level this reads as 123 / 5 / 18 rows for Stim8hr / Rest / Stim48hr — the counts `regulator_score`
  uses — but 98 of the 123 Stim8hr rows come from a single large module, cluster 88, so the row-gap
  reflects module size, not a 7× concentration; excluding it leaves 25 vs 18, near-equal.)

→ `figures/fig3_de_direction_by_condition.png`

## 5. From regulators to autoimmune disease

The screen's downstream (trans-affected) genes were clustered into 112 gene-regulatory modules;
each module's downstream program was tested for enrichment of autoimmune-disease genes. Joining
each regulator to its module and that module's enrichment yields **146 gene × condition rows in
autoimmune-enriched modules** (odds ratio > 1, FDR < 0.1). The most strongly enriched modules —
**cluster 89** (OR 3.0, FDR 5×10⁻⁶), **cluster 59** (OR 2.5), **cluster 54** (OR 2.3, 43
member genes) — are currently annotated "unknown," i.e. **novel disease-relevant regulatory
programs** rather than textbook complexes. This directly operationalizes the "perturbation →
disease" thesis of the dataset and mirrors the Freimer et al. (Nat Genet 2022) template of
building disease-enriched T cell GRNs from perturbation trans-effects.

→ `figures/fig5_autoimmune_clusters.png`

## 6. How the score works

`regulator_score` (0–9) is a **transparent additive heuristic, not a statistical model** —
reweight it from the component columns for your use case:

```
regulator_score =
    is_strong_regulator (>10 DE genes)      × 2
  + ontarget_significant                          +1
  + is_efficient_kd (≥50% guides sig. KD)         +1
  + is_reproducible_donor (cross-donor r ≥ 0.4)   +1
  + is_reproducible_guide (cross-guide r ≥ 0.4)   +1
  + is_polarization_regulator (coef_rank ≥ 0.9 or known)  +1
  + is_aging_regulator (coef_rank ≥ 0.9 or known)         +1
  + is_autoimmune_enriched (module OR>1 & FDR<0.1)        +1
```

Every term is an independent evidence layer, so the score rewards **convergence**; every term is
a plain column comparison, so it is fully recomputable. → `figures/fig2_evidence_coverage.png`

## 7. How to use this dataset

- **Target triage:** open `data/integrated/regulator_master.csv`, filter to one condition, sort
  by `regulator_score`; filter `is_polarization_regulator & is_autoimmune_enriched` for the
  convergent, disease-relevant polarization regulators.
- **Per-condition strategy:** `data/integrated/condition_summary.csv`.
- **Module exploration:** `data/integrated/cluster_summary.csv`; load
  `figures/regulator_module_network.graphml` into Cytoscape/Gephi.
- **Benchmark baseline:** `scripts/perturbation_baseline.py` scores how well one context's / one
  cell type's perturbation effects predict another's (see §8).

## 8. A predictive baseline (cz-benchmarks idiom)

`scripts/perturbation_baseline.py` reimplements the CZI cz-benchmarks *Perturbation Expression
Prediction* metric — Spearman correlation of predicted vs ground-truth per-gene log-fold-change —
for three trivial, model-free predictors computed directly from the genome-wide DE matrix
(`GWCD4i.DE_stats.h5ad`, 33,983 × 10,282). They are the floor any perturbation-prediction model —
including the dataset's own **scLDM.CD4** generative model — must beat, and they quantify
context-specificity:

| Baseline | Metric | Result (median) | vs null |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A. Cross-condition transfer** (predict one condition's per-gene log-FC from another's) | Spearman ρ | Rest→Stim8hr **0.225**, Stim8hr→Stim48hr **0.210**, Rest→Stim48hr **0.195** | random pairing ρ ≈ 0.01 |
| **B. Condition-mean floor** (predict every perturbation by its condition's mean vector) | Spearman ρ | Rest 0.10, Stim8hr 0.12, Stim48hr 0.15 | — |
| **C. Cross-cell-type** K562→CD4 (shipped comparison) | Pearson r | Rest 0.30, Stim8hr 0.32, Stim48hr 0.31 | random perturbation r ≈ 0.004 |

**Reading:** perturbation effects transfer across stimulation conditions **only modestly (ρ ≈ 0.2)
but ~20× above chance**, and transfer *degrades with context distance* — the most dissimilar pair,
Rest→Stim48hr, is the lowest (0.195) — a direct, quantitative confirmation of the paper's
context-specificity thesis. That per-perturbation transfer (≈ 0.2) is roughly double the generic
condition-mean floor (≈ 0.1), so there is real perturbation-specific signal to predict. Effects are
comparatively more conserved *across cell types* (K562→CD4 r ≈ 0.31 vs ~0.004 random) — a large
shared regulatory component. (A/B are Spearman over all genes; C is the shipped Pearson on the
K562-overlap set, so absolute values are not directly comparable.)

*Honesty note:* the actual `czbenchmarks` Task object also needs raw single cells for GEM/UMI
control-matching, absent from this processed release — this is a faithful re-implementation of the
*metric*, not a claim of running the shipped Task.
→ `figures/fig7_baseline_spearman.png`, `data/integrated/perturbation_baseline_summary.csv`.

## 9. Caveats

- The source is a **December 2025 preprint**; "novel" regulator framing is the authors'.
- The shipped `DE_stats.suppl_table.csv` is a **reduced** per-perturbation summary; cross-guide
  and cross-donor reproducibility come from the repo's dedicated correlation tables and cover
  only perturbations tested with ≥2 guides / ≥2 donors — absence means "not measured here," not
  "not reproducible."
- **scLDM.CD4 is an unbenchmarked, developer-intended-use model** (GPU-only, single-gene
  knockdowns); treat its rankings as a prioritization aid to be validated, not ground truth.
- Arrayed protein-level validation here covers only IL10/IL21 (13 perturbations) — a small
  spot-check, not a genome-wide validation layer.
- The `regulator_score` thresholds are transparent choices at the top of `build_dataset.py`;
  reweight for your question.
